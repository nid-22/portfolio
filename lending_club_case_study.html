General Information

You are working for a consumer finance company that specializes in lending various types of loans to urban customers.

When the company receives a loan application, it must decide whether to approve the loan based on the applicantâ€™s profile.

  • If the applicant is likely to repay the loan, not approving the loan results in a loss of business for the company.
  • If the applicant is not likely to repay (i.e., likely to default), approving the loan may lead to a financial loss for the company.

The data provided contains information about past loan applicants and whether they defaulted or not.

The aim is to identify patterns that indicate if a person is likely to default. This can help in taking actions such as denying the loan, reducing the loan amount, or lending to risky applicants at a higher interest rate.

In this case study, you will use Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand how consumer and loan attributes influence the tendency to default.

When a person applies for a loan, the company can take two types of decisions:

  • Loan accepted: If the company approves the loan, there are three possible scenarios:
    • Fully paid: Applicant has fully paid the loan (principal and interest).
    • Current: Applicant is still paying installments; the loan tenure is not yet completed. These are not labeled as 'defaulted'.
    • Charged-off: Applicant has not paid installments for a long period, i.e., has defaulted on the loan.
  • Loan rejected: The company rejected the loan (e.g., the candidate does not meet requirements). There is no transactional history for these applicants, so this data is not available in the dataset.

Business Objectives

The company is the largest online loan marketplace, facilitating personal loans, business loans, and financing of medical procedures. Borrowers can access lower interest rate loans through a fast online interface.

Like most lending companies, lending to risky applicants is the largest source of financial loss (called credit loss). Credit loss is the amount lost when a borrower refuses to pay or runs away with the money owed. In this case, customers labeled as 'charged-off' are the defaulters.

If risky loan applicants can be identified, such loans can be reduced, thereby cutting down credit loss. Identification of such applicants using EDA is the aim of this case study.

The company wants to understand the driving factors (driver variables) behind loan default, i.e., variables that are strong indicators of default. This knowledge can be used for portfolio and risk assessment.

To develop your understanding of the domain, you are advised to research risk analytics (understanding the types of variables and their significance should be enough).

Conclusions

  • Grades D, E, F, and G are charged off more than other grades.
  • Higher installment amounts are charged off more frequently.
  • Higher interest rates over a period of 60 months are charged off more compared to lower interest rates.
  • Lower income and high installments cause a higher percentage of charge-offs.
