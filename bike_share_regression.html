Check on Github

Objective: Model the demand for shared bikes using available independent variables. This helps management understand how demand varies with different features.

Problem Statement

A bike-sharing system is a service where bikes are made available for shared use to individuals on a short-term basis, either for a price or for free. Many systems allow users to borrow a bike from a computer-controlled "dock" after entering payment information. The bike can then be returned to any other dock in the system.

BoomBikes, a US-based bike-sharing provider, has recently experienced a significant drop in revenue due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The company is struggling to sustain itself in the current market scenario. To address this, BoomBikes aims to develop a strategic business plan to accelerate revenue as soon as the lockdown ends and the economy recovers.

As part of this plan, BoomBikes wants to understand the demand for shared bikes after the quarantine ends. This will help them prepare to meet customer needs, outperform competitors, and maximize profits as the situation improves.

The Company Wants to Know

  1. Which variables are significant in predicting the demand for shared bikes?
  2. How well do these variables explain the bike demand?
